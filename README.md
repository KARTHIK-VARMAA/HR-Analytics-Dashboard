HR Analytics Dashboard – Employee Attrition Analysis
Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition patterns and workforce trends using SQL and Power BI. The objective was to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable insights that can help organizations improve employee retention strategies.

The analysis was performed on the IBM HR Analytics dataset containing 1,470 employee records.

Objectives
Analyze workforce demographics and employee distribution.
Measure employee attrition and retention trends.
Identify key drivers contributing to employee turnover.
Build an interactive dashboard for HR decision-making.
Generate business insights to support employee retention strategies.

Key Business Insights
Department Analysis
Sales department recorded the highest attrition rate at 20.63%.
Research & Development accounted for the highest number of attrition cases due to its larger workforce size.
Business Travel Analysis
Employees who travel frequently experienced an attrition rate of 24.9%.
Non-travel employees showed the lowest attrition rate at approximately 8%.
Education Analysis
Employees from Human Resources, Marketing, and Technical Degree backgrounds exhibited higher attrition rates compared to other education groups.
Workforce Insights
Average Employee Age: 36.9 years
Research & Development is the largest department with 961 employees.
Life Sciences is the most common employee education background.
Business Recommendations
Implement retention programs for Sales employees.
Reduce excessive travel requirements where possible.
Monitor high-risk employee groups with elevated attrition rates.
Improve employee engagement and career development initiatives.
Conduct regular attrition reviews using dashboard insights.
